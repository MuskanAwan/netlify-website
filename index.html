RELIGION OF ISLAM
    The five pillars of Islam, or "arkan al-Islam," are the five basic acts that are considered mandatory for all Muslims. They are:
  1. Shahada: Declaration of faith, stating that there is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is his messenger.
  2. Salat: Performing the five daily prayers facing the Kaaba in Mecca.
  3. Sawm: Fasting during the month of Ramadan, abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs from dawn to sunset.
  4. Zakat: Giving charity to those in need, typically calculated as 2.5% of one's wealth.
  5. Hajj: Making the pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca at least once in a lifetime, if physically and financially able.



TUHEED
"Toheed e Islam" is a fundamental concept in Islam, emphasizing the belief in the oneness of Allah. It encompasses the belief in one God, His uniqueness, and the rejection of any partners or intermediaries. This concept is central to Islamic theology and shapes the faith's practices and worldview.



NAMAZ
Namaz, also known as Salah, is one of the five pillars of Islam. It is a ritual prayer performed by Muslims five times a day: at dawn (Fajr), midday (Dhuhr), mid-afternoon (Asr), sunset (Maghrib), and evening (Isha). Namaz involves specific physical postures and recitations, serving as a direct means of communication between the individual and Allah. It is a fundamental practice that fosters spiritual growth, discipline, and mindfulness in the life of a Muslim.



ROZA
Islamic lunar calendar, holds great significance for Muslims worldwide. It is a month of fasting, prayer, reflection, and community. Observant Muslims fast from dawn until sunset, abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs. The fast is broken each evening with a meal called iftar. Ramadan is also a time for increased devotion, charity, and self-discipline, as well as for strengthening bonds with family and community members. It concludes with the celebration of Eid al-Fitr, a joyous festival marking the end of fasting and the beginning of festivities.



ZAKAT
Zakat is a fundamental pillar of Islam, obligating Muslims to give a portion of their wealth to those in need, serving as a form of social welfare and purification of wealth. It's typically calculated as 2.5% of a Muslim's savings and investments after meeting basic needs, and it's considered a religious duty and an act of worship.



HAJJ
Hajj is the pilgrimage to Mecca, one of the Five Pillars of Islam, obligatory for all Muslims who are physically and financially able to undertake it at least once in their lifetime. It symbolizes the unity of Muslims worldwide and commemorates the actions of Prophet Ibrahim and his family. The rituals performed during Hajj are deeply spiritual and connect participants to their faith and the history of Islam.
